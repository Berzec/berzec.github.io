The EU Governing Body composes of seven different institutions, all of which provide a different service. It has the basic three branches of a government: the legislative branch, the executive branch, and the judicial branch. To see more about the EU government, see the EU Types of Institutions and Bodies.

The Berlaymont, headquarters of the EU, in Brussels, Belgium

EU citizens are entitled to the same basic rights and freedoms as most other countries. EU citizens are also granted freedom of movement between each member state, with no checkpoints or passports needed. For more information see the EU's Principles and Values.
